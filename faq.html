Gas is essential to process requests on the blockchain. In this sense, even a failed transaction still represents a transaction. This fee is payed to the Ethereum network and miners, and not to WallOfChain.

It’s possible that the Ethereum network may be too busy to process the transaction. It usually happens when gas prices are too high, or there is a spike in network congestion.

Gas is a short term used to describe the cost of a transaction or contract in Ethereum. Each transaction takes a lot of computational power, and is splitted beneath multiple computers. This power is covered by gas.
